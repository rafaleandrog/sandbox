Tipolis Strategic Program — City Bonds

From Institutional Capital to City Bonds

1. Context and Strategic Framing

Tipolis structures International Cities through SAR frameworks and project-level SPVs with host governments, isolating project debt from sovereign and Tipolis parent balance sheets. City Bonds are built as private capital instruments to fund real estate, infrastructure, and urban systems at scale, with a dual-return profile: operating yield plus structured Bitcoin-linked optionality.

2. Revenue Bond Analogy

City Bonds are designed as municipal-style revenue bonds: repayment capacity is tied to asset-generated cash flows (rent, concession, service fees), not sovereign guarantee. Two innovations are layered in: a 10% BTC reserve allocation and a recurring programmatic issuance architecture.

3. Capital Flow Structure
Step 1

Investor subscribes in USD.

Step 2

Issuance by Tipolis / SPV / Hybrid model under analysis.

Step 3

90% deployed to city assets (9% cap-rate assumption).

Step 4

10% BTC reserve allocated at issuance date.

Step 5

Monthly distributions + maturity settlement by instrument terms.

4. Product Suite
CB6

20-year, senior secured, 6% coupon, monthly payments, principal at maturity + 50% BTC appreciation on 10% notional.

CB10

10-year, subordinated, 10% coupon, monthly payments, principal at maturity + 50% BTC appreciation on 10% notional.

CIP

Perpetual preferred equity hybrid, 4% floor (monthly) + project equity participation. No BTC sharing commitment to investor.

5. Core Constants Across Issuances
  • Operational allocation: 90% into city assets.
  • Bitcoin reserve: 10% allocated at issuance.
  • Ramp period: Years 1–3 with no operating income; monthly obligations start from month 1.
  • Payment cadence: Monthly for all instruments.

7. Issuance Entity Analysis (Open Decision)

Option A (Tipolis parent): consolidated track record and wider investor access; higher balance-sheet and regulatory consolidation risk.

Option B (SPV-level): ring-fenced project risk and recognized revenue-bond treatment; higher cold-start credit friction per SPV.

Option C (Hybrid): SPV issuance for CB products + parent-level CIP issuance under evaluation; final legal structure pending.

8. Investor Fit

CB6 targets SWFs, pension funds, insurers, and investment-grade infrastructure mandates. CB10 targets high-yield funds, private credit, and BTC-conviction allocators needing income + asymmetry. CIP targets long-horizon investors seeking perpetual income floor + real estate equity-linked upside.
